Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about our accounting services

What documents do I need for tax preparation?

You'll need your T4 slips from employers, T5 slips for investment income, receipts for deductible expenses, RRSP contribution receipts, medical receipts, charitable donation receipts, and any other relevant tax documents. We'll provide you with a comprehensive checklist when you book your appointment.

How much do your services cost?

Our fees vary based on the complexity of your tax situation. Personal tax returns start at $75, while business tax returns begin at $250. We provide upfront pricing with no hidden fees. Contact us for a free consultation and personalized quote.

Do you offer services year-round or just during tax season?

We offer comprehensive accounting services year-round! While tax preparation is busy from February to April, we provide bookkeeping, payroll, HST filing, financial planning, and business consulting services throughout the entire year.

Can you help with CRA audits and reviews?

Yes, we provide full support for CRA audits, reviews, and correspondence. Our experienced team will represent you, gather required documentation, and communicate with the CRA on your behalf to resolve any issues efficiently.

Do you work with small businesses and self-employed individuals?

Absolutely! We specialize in helping small businesses, freelancers, and self-employed individuals. Our services include business registration, bookkeeping, payroll setup, HST registration and filing, and strategic tax planning to minimize your tax burden.

How long does it take to complete my tax return?

Most personal tax returns are completed within 3-5 business days, while business returns typically take 7-10 business days. During peak tax season (March-April), there may be slight delays. We always provide realistic timelines when you drop off your documents.

Do you offer electronic filing and direct deposit?

Yes! We file all tax returns electronically with the CRA, which means faster processing and quicker refunds. If you're entitled to a refund, you can receive it via direct deposit, typically within 8-14 days of filing.

What if I missed the tax filing deadline?

Don't worry! We can help you file late returns and minimize penalties. While there may be late-filing penalties and interest charges, we'll work to reduce these costs and get you back in good standing with the CRA as quickly as possible.
